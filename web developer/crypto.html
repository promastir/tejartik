What is a digital currency?

The digital currency is secured by cryptography so it is practically impossible for fraud to occur in its transactions. The latter problem, which does not exist with traditional currencies, involves the risk of double spending of a single digital currency. Certainly, it is a potential and different obstacle for digital currencies, relatively digital information can be easily copied from individuals (albeit unknown) who understand the blockchain network and the computer software needed to control it.


futuer of digital currency

The past period has seen a major shift in digital currencies, as their growth has been faster than ever, yet their future has never been clear, as many people entered cryptocurrency trading for the first time during the Corona pandemic due to the lack of activities on which they can spend money.

The trend towards digital currencies that were previously understood only among a small community of investors has increased, but now it has become a household name for everyone, and analysts estimate that the global cryptocurrency market will triple by 2030, reaching a valuation of nearly 5 billion US dollars, investors, companies and brands cannot ignore the rising tide of cryptocurrencies for a long time, whether they want to buy or not.



the best 10 digital currency

1- bitcoin

Bitcoin is the world's most popular digital currency with origins dating back to 2009; it remains the highest cryptocurrency by market capitalization today. Bitcoin operates under its blockchain umbrella, where transactions are verified, and new bitcoins are created up to a fixed limit, by a large number of decentralized miners. In January 2023, Bitcoin had the largest market capitalization of $326 billion.

2- Ethereum

Ethereum is the cryptocurrency that runs on the Ethereum blockchain. Like Bitcoin, Ethereum runs on its own blockchain, but unlike Bitcoin, Ethereum has no cap, which means it theoretically can create an infinite amount of currency. Ethereum also supports smart contracts. These are programs that run on their own blockchain technology that run automatically when certain conditions are met.Ethereum's market value as of the time of writing is $154 billion.

3- Tether

Tether is a type of stablecoin whose price is said to be less volatile because it is pegged to an offshore asset. In this case, each currency is backed by an amount equivalent to the US dollar, resulting in it not being exposed to the same price fluctuations as other cryptocurrencies. It has a market capitalization of $66 billion.

4- litcoin

Litcoin is a form of cryptocurrency, which can be used to buy and sell online and transfer money between accounts. So it's similar to Bitcoin in the value it adds, but it's unlikely to surpass it, although it has introduced a number of improvements such as reducing block creation time, thus making transactions faster. This is because Bitcoin is the first cryptocurrency to emerge and as it is most popular among investors and traders.

5- Binance

It is the official currency of the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange in 2021. For users who pay in BNB, transaction fees for this exchange are reduced. This led to the adoption of Binance Coin, making it one of the largest cryptocurrencies on the market right now. To ensure value stability, Binance is destroying or "burning" a fixed percentage of coins in circulation.The market capitalization is $42 billion as of January 2023.

6- Ripple

Ripple is described as a "banking cryptocurrency" because it is designed to meet the needs of the financial services industry. Ripple is meant to be a means of enabling international payments and acts as a bridge between two different currencies to offer cheaper and faster global transfers. It has a market capitalization of $17 billion.

7- Binance USD

It is a 1:1 USD backed stablecoin issued by the Binance Exchange, approved and regulated by the New York State Department of Financial Services. Launched on September 5, 2019, BUSD aims to integrate dollar stability with blockchain technology. It currently has a market capitalization of $16 billion.

8- Cardano

This digital currency is called the "third generation cryptocurrency", as it divides the blockchain into two levels to increase the speed of transactions, and also implements native tokens to provide a better experience for coin holders. Cardano has a market value of $10 billion.

9- Dogecoin

This currency is based on the famous Internet meme "doge". The open-source digital currency was created by Billy Marcus of Portland and Oregon and Jackson Palmer of Sydney, Australia, and was dismantled from Litecoin in December 2013. The creators of Dogecoin envisioned it as a fun and light cryptocurrency that would have more appeal after coin. Elon Musk, Tesla CEO, posted several tweets on social media stating that Dogecoin is his currency of choice.Dogecoin has a market value of $9 billion.

10- Polygon

It is the first well-structured and easy-to-use platform for scaling Ethereum and developing infrastructure. Its core component is the Polygon SDK, a modular and flexible framework that supports the construction of multiple types of applications.The coin effectively transforms Ethereum into an integrated multi-chain system, aka blockchain internet. This multi-chain system is similar to other systems such as Polkadot, Cosmos, Avalanche, etc. with Ethereum security advantages. It has a market capitalization of $7 billion.


Cryptocurrency Risks

Interest in the digital currency has recently increased and a group of the major company has started dealing with it, however, there are still a lot of concerns about that currency, both in terms of security and regarding the legal implications of this currency

Decentralized Cryptocurrency Status

One of the great attractions of many cryptocurrencies is also a potential risk factor for the individual investor, and Bitcoin has paved the way for other cryptocurrencies in terms of being decentralized, Which means that it has no physical presence and is not supported by a central authority, while governments around the world have stepped in to assert their regulatory authority in various ways, Bitcoin and other digital currencies like it remain untied to any jurisdiction or institution, on the one hand, this frees investors from being indebted to those institutions, but on the other hand, The value of digital currencies depends entirely on the value attributed to them by owners and other investors; this is true in all currencies, digital or fidatory, but without a central authority supporting the value of the digital currency, investors may be in trouble if complications arise in transactions or ownership.

Price change risk

The price of cryptocurrencies fluctuates constantly, the price of cryptocurrency trading can suddenly rise or fall suddenly, and there is a possibility that the value of the cryptocurrency will fall to zero, and this is something you should keep in mind when buying.

Risks of working hours

The price of cryptocurrencies can flip at any time, and the markets for buying and selling cryptocurrencies may operate at specific times and close at specific times, so you cannot trade on those currencies for the hours when the markets close, regardless of the size of the change in the price of the currency.

Risk of losing the password for your wallet

If you lose your wallet password, you may not be able to access your money again.
